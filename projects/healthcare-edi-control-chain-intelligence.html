Executive breakdown

EDI is the standardized administrative language between healthcare systems.

Enrollment and eligibility

The 834 maintains enrollment data. The 270 asks about eligibility and benefits; the 271 returns the payer's response.

Authorization

The X12 278 supports service-review, referral, and prior-authorization requests and responses; FHIR APIs expand this area for 2027.

Claims and acknowledgments

The 837 submits the claim. The 999 and 277CA help distinguish structural acceptance from claim-level acceptance or rejection.

Status and remittance

The 276/277 supports claim-status follow-up. The 835 explains payment, adjustments, denials, and patient responsibility.

End-to-end control chain

The visible problem may occur several transactions after the first control loss.

01

Enroll

834 exceptions are assigned and aged.

02

Verify

270/271 confirms coverage and benefits.

03

08

Reconcile

835 and EFT are matched, posted, corrected, and closed.

Simulated case

The coverage update that never reached the patient workflow.

A member-level 834 maintenance record failed validation and entered an unowned exception queue. Days later, the 271 response showed inactive coverage, the authorization request could not match the member, and the patient's imaging service was delayed.

Visible issueInactive eligibility and delayed authorization at patient access.First loss of controlAn 834 enrollment exception had no owner, due date, aging standard, escalation, or proof of reprocessing.Patient impactRepeated calls, uncertainty about coverage, delayed scheduling, and fear of unexpected cost.Operational responseAssign exceptions, create a discrepancy workflow, validate authorization identity, review acknowledgments, and verify downstream correction.
