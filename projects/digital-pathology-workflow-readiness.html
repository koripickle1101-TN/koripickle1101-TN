Executive breakdown

Digital pathology is a workflow transformation—not simply a slide scanner.

Glass slides become digital cases

Whole-slide imaging creates high-resolution digital images that must remain connected to the correct accession, specimen, block, slide, stain, review queue, report, and archive record.

Quality is multi-layered

Identity, tissue capture, focus, color, orientation, metadata, file integrity, display, access, and routing all affect whether the case is ready for professional review.

Interoperability matters

LIS, scanners, image-management platforms, viewers, storage, reports, and authorized AI applications may use different formats and mappings that require controlled integration.

AI remains task-specific

A base whole-slide imaging workflow should not be assumed to include authorized diagnostic AI. Intended use, human review, validation, and monitoring remain essential.

Simulated case

The complete scan that never became a complete digital case.

A hospital activates a new whole-slide imaging workflow. A slide scans successfully, but the scanner interface truncates part of the accession identifier. The image is stored without attaching to the correct digital case. The technical exception queue has no assigned owner, aging threshold, or escalation.

Visible issueThe pathologist opens an incomplete case and the report is delayed.First loss of controlLIS-to-scanner metadata mapping went live without a hard reconciliation rule for accession, block, slide, and stain identifiers.Why it was missedThe scanner reported technical completion, but no control verified attachment to the correct case and active review queue.Patient impactPossible delayed diagnosis, postponed follow-up, repeat contact, and uncertainty about next steps.Operational responseIdentity reconciliation, image-QC gates, business acknowledgments, owned exceptions, downtime procedures, synthetic UAT, KPIs, and verified closure.
Operational controls

From scan completion to case completion.

Identity reconciliation

Match accession, specimen or block, slide, and stain identifiers before the image becomes review-ready.

Image-quality checkpoint

Confirm complete tissue capture, focus, color, orientation, file integrity, and expected image count.

Exception ownership

Assign metadata, image-quality, routing, and interface failures to named queues, due dates, and escalation paths.

Closed-loop verification

Confirm that image, review status, final report, downstream routing, and archive record all agree.

Technical scan success is not operational success. The image must arrive in the correct case, queue, review environment, and final record.
