Kori Pickle · BSHA Candidate
Executive breakdown

Medicare has four parts, but they are not four equivalent plans.

Original Medicare

Includes Part A and Part B. A separate Part D plan may be added for prescription drug coverage.

Medicare Advantage

Part C is a private-plan alternative for receiving Part A and Part B benefits and usually includes Part D. Claims go to the plan, not the Original Medicare fee-for-service route.

Part A and status

Part A generally supports inpatient hospital, skilled nursing facility, hospice, and some home health care. Observation remains outpatient unless a formal inpatient order is documented.

Part B and claim streams

Part B supports professional and outpatient services. Institutional versus professional claim forms are not a simple Part A-versus-Part B distinction.

Part identification is only the beginning. The operational skill is proving which coverage pathway, payer, status, evidence, and claim route apply to the specific service and date.

Simulated case

One Medicare patient. Four pathways. One false-ready status.

A simulated hospital scheduling team uses one status called “Medicare verified.” The patient is scheduled for outpatient imaging, receives a professional interpretation, may need durable medical equipment, and takes a high-cost outpatient prescription. Eligibility is confirmed, but the workflow does not identify the full coverage arrangement or separate the required pathways.

Part A / statusAn overnight observation stay is assumed to be inpatient because the patient remained in a hospital bed.Part B / claim streamsFacility and professional charges are not separated into distinct work paths with their own evidence and payer routes.Part C / payer routingThe patient is enrolled in Medicare Advantage, but the account is routed as Original Medicare fee-for-service.Part D / drug coverageThe plan formulary and prior-authorization requirement are not reviewed before the prescription reaches the pharmacy.First loss of control“Medicare verified” was treated as proof of patient status, payer route, claim type, network, authorization, drug coverage, and readiness.

Synthetic UAT

Test the exceptions before a patient or claim absorbs the defect.

Medicare Advantage route

System sends the claim to the MA plan and blocks Original Medicare submission.

Observation overnight

System preserves outpatient/observation status unless a formal inpatient order is present.

Facility + professional

System creates distinct institutional and professional work paths before closure.

Other coverage

System blocks claim-ready status until payer order is resolved and evidenced.

Part D exception

System records formulary status, utilization management, owner, and next action.

Coverage change

A new plan resets dependent statuses while preserving the previous audit trail.
