Executive breakdown

What the 837 and 835 mean operationally.

837 claim

The outbound structured claim transaction carrying patient, provider, diagnosis, procedure, date, charge, and required identifier information to a payer.

Payer adjudication

The decision process applying eligibility, benefits, coverage, coding, medical policy, coordination-of-benefits, and contract logic.

835 ERA

The payment and remittance advice transaction explaining payment, adjustments, denials, and patient responsibility.

Posting + reconciliation

The provider-side work of matching money and remittance detail, interpreting responsibility, posting accurately, routing exceptions, and proving closure.

Simulated no-PHI case

Balanced dollars. Wrong responsibility.

A hospital submits a valid institutional claim. The payer sends a balanced 835. During an internal mapping update, a contractual adjustment is mistakenly categorized as patient responsibility. Because the total dollars still reconcile, a basic balance check does not stop the posting.

BILLED$4,200
PAID$2,750
CONTRACTUAL$1,150
PATIENT$300

The account displayed $1,450 as patient responsibility.

The ledger balanced, but accountability was wrong. A technical mapping defect became a patient-facing financial risk.

Visible issuePatient account and statement-generation workflow.
First loss of controlInbound 835 mapping change released without responsibility-code validation.
Why it was missedTesting checked total dollars but not code meaning or patient-balance variance.
Who is affectedPatient, payment-posting staff, call center, revenue integrity, and implementation support.
Control response

Protect both the patient and the revenue.

1

837 readiness

Verify identity, coverage, authorization or referral status, documentation, charges, codes, and required identifiers before transmission.

2

EDI acceptance

Track acknowledgments, separate structural rejection from adjudicated denial, and assign ownership for rejected claims.

3

835 interpretation

Validate responsibility-code mapping and test synthetic “golden” cases with known expected results before release.

4

Posting + closed loop

Hold unusual patient balances, route exceptions, document correction, and verify account and statement status after resolution.

Interview explanation

How I describe the project

“I built a simulated 837-to-835 case study to understand how a claim moves from upstream readiness through adjudication and payment posting. I focused on a scenario where the dollars balanced but the financial-responsibility mapping was wrong. That showed me why workflow controls must validate meaning, ownership, and patient impact—not just totals. The project does not represent employer experience, but it demonstrates how I trace where control first failed and design role-appropriate controls.”
