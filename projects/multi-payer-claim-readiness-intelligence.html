CORE QUESTION

Kori Pickle · BSHA Candidate
Executive breakdown

Correct coding cannot repair an upstream payer-readiness failure.

Medicare

Requires the correct Medicare pathway, payer-order determination when other coverage exists, coverage criteria, documentation, and appropriate claim routing.

Medicaid

Combines federal requirements with state administration. Managed care can add exact-plan networks, authorization rules, manuals, and contract-based workflows.

Commercial

Uses private plan contracts, products, benefit designs, networks, utilization-management policies, and payer-specific submission requirements.

Shared risk

One vague “insurance verified” status can hide unresolved eligibility, benefits, payer order, network, authorization, documentation, and routing decisions.

Knowing what is being billed matters. Knowing which payer logic governs the claim matters just as much.

Simulated case

One outpatient service. Three payer paths. Three different risks.

Synthetic UAT

Test the exceptions before a real patient or claim absorbs the defect.

Medicare secondary

System blocks Medicare-primary status until other coverage and payer order are resolved.

Medicaid plan mismatch

State eligibility can remain active while exact-plan authorization and routing remain unresolved.

Commercial network gap

Overall readiness stays open when facility or ancillary network status is unknown.

Wrong authorization scope

Approval for the wrong date, provider, service, or units does not satisfy readiness.

Coverage change trigger

A plan change resets dependent statuses while preserving the previous audit trail.

Conflict handling

Contradictory portal and phone responses create an owned exception instead of a silent guess.

Measurement

KPIs that show control before denials accumulate.

Exact payer identification

Accounts with program, exact plan, product, line of business, and payer ID confirmed.

Payer-order resolution

Multi-coverage accounts with primary and secondary responsibility resolved before claim creation.

Authorization evidence

Required approvals with service, dates, units, provider, and reference captured.

Network uncertainty aging

Open time for unresolved provider, facility, or ancillary network status.

First-pass acceptance

Claims accepted without front-end payer, routing, or format correction.

Preventable recurrence

Repeat denials linked to the same payer-rule root cause after a control change.
