CORE QUESTION

Where did the workflow first lose control?

Kori Pickle · BSHA Candidate
Executive breakdown

AI inside the workflow is useful only when the workflow remains controlled.

Word and PowerPoint

Copilot can support first drafts, rewriting, summaries, presentations, and structured content. Human reviewers still verify facts, scope, citations, audience fit, and final approval.

Excel

Copilot can help explore data, create formulas, charts, PivotTables, and summaries. Source quality, filters, denominators, formulas, and conclusions still require validation.

Outlook and Teams

Copilot can summarize messages and meetings, draft responses, and identify action items. Users must confirm recipients, commitments, decisions, owners, dates, and sensitive content.

Healthcare boundary

Only organization-approved accounts, environments, data, and policies should be used. This portfolio project uses simulated no-PHI content and does not claim clinical or compliance authority.

Simulated case

The meeting summary that looked complete but lost operational control.

A simulated healthcare operations team uses Copilot to convert a Teams transcript and a no-PHI Excel backlog into an action plan. The generated summary merges two decisions, assigns the wrong owner, omits a data-quality dependency, and creates a deadline that was never approved.

Visible issueThe wrong team begins work while the actual dependency remains unresolved.First loss of controlThe organization treated a generated summary as an approved record without source-linked review, owner confirmation, or decision-log reconciliation.Patient and staff riskIncorrect ownership can delay access, create rework, produce conflicting communication, and hide unresolved workflow barriers.Operational responseControlled source packages, structured prompts, independent verification, approval gates, synthetic UAT, privacy boundaries, KPI measurement, and verified closure.
