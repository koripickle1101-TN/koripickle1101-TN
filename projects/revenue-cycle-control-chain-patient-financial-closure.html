Executive breakdown

Revenue cycle management is a connected control chain, not one department.

Front end

Patient identity, demographics, payer order, eligibility, benefits, network, authorization, estimates, and patient communication create the foundation for every stage that follows.

Mid cycle

Documentation, authorized coding inputs, charge capture, reconciliation, and claim edits determine whether the payment request accurately represents the encounter.

Back end

Claim status, adjudication, remittance, payment posting, denials, underpayments, patient balances, refunds, and disputes require separate ownership and evidence.

Closed-loop prevention

Denial and payment information must feed upstream controls so the same registration, authorization, documentation, charge, or routing failure does not repeat.

A stage is complete only when its output is usable by the next owner and its unresolved risk remains visible.

Simulated case

One outpatient service. Six disconnected control failures.

A simulated patient is scheduled for advanced outpatient imaging. The organization uses separate scheduling, eligibility, authorization, clinical documentation, billing, and payment-posting workflows. Each team completes its local task, but no shared control confirms that the same patient, plan, service, location, date, units, and financial assumptions remain aligned.

RegistrationThe new plan is added, but the old payer remains primary.AuthorizationApproval was obtained for the original facility before the appointment moved.Charge captureA contrast supply unit is duplicated during manual reconciliation.Claim outcomeThe wrong payer receives the claim first; the corrected claim is denied for authorization-location mismatch.Payment postingThe denial amount moves to patient responsibility before final internal review.First loss of controlThe coverage and location changes did not reset dependent authorization, network, estimate, routing, and posting statuses.
Closed-loop redesign

Eight controls from identity to verified closure.

1. Identify and validate

Confirm the patient, encounter, exact payer, payer order, service, provider, facility, date, communication needs, eligibility, benefits, and network.

2. Authorize and reconcile

Match approval evidence to the final service, provider, facility, dates, units, documentation, orders, charges, and quantities.

3. Submit and monitor

Release the correct claim to the correct payer, preserve submission evidence, monitor acknowledgments, claim status, aging, information requests, and timely-filing risk.

4. Resolve and close

Correct rejections, classify denials, review payment variances, protect patient balances, reconcile remittance and posting, communicate clearly, and update upstream controls.

The cycle closes with verified responsibility, accurate communication, and prevention feedback - not merely with a posted transaction.

Synthetic UAT and KPIs

Test the handoffs before a patient or claim absorbs the defect.

Coverage change

Dependent authorization, network, estimate, payer-order, and routing statuses reopen while prior evidence remains visible.

Facility change

Location-dependent network and authorization controls reset automatically.

Duplicate charge

Quantity reconciliation blocks claim-ready status until the variance is resolved.

Claim rejection

An owner, correction deadline, timely-filing risk, and resubmission evidence are required.

Denial to patient balance

Unresolved payer responsibility remains on internal hold until review is complete.

Information request

The request enters an exception queue with source, due date, approved document route, owner, and closure proof.
