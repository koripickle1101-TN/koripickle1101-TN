CORE QUESTION

Kori Pickle · BSHA Candidate
Breakdown

The post is directionally right, but the operational lesson is deeper.

What it gets right

Medicaid is administered by states under federal requirements. Managed-care organizations can add plan-specific provider processes, networks, utilization controls, portals, vendors, and claim instructions.

What it oversimplifies

Active Medicaid enrollment does not automatically prove the exact health plan, service benefit, network status, referral, authorization, COB/TPL, claim route, or filing rule.

Why coding is not the whole answer

A correctly coded claim can still be denied when an upstream requirement was unresolved. Coding may be accurate while eligibility, authorization, network, documentation, or payer routing is not.

The professional takeaway

Treat Medicaid as a rule-source workflow. Record the current source, date, evidence, status, unresolved question, next owner, and recheck trigger.

A clean claim does not start with one verification checkbox. It starts with exact-plan identification, service-specific evidence, ownership, and verified closure.

Simulated case M-014

The denial appeared downstream. The first loss of control occurred before the visit.

A simulated imaging visit is marked ready after staff confirm “TennCare active.” The exact MCO is not captured, service-specific benefits are not reviewed, and no separate authorization status or owner is required. The managed plan later denies the claim for missing authorization evidence.

Testing and measurement

Synthetic UAT and KPI proof.

UAT scenarios

Wrong MCO selected; service requires authorization; plan changes before service; other insurance exists; portal response is incomplete; denial returns for missing authorization.

Expected system behavior

Block false readiness, assign an owner and deadline, cancel stale evidence, route COB/TPL, preserve an unresolved status, and return denial findings upstream.

Leading indicators

Exact-plan identification rate, complete readiness rate, exception aging, re-verification catch rate, and patient communication documentation.

Closed-loop indicators

Preventable Medicaid denials traced to the originating control, corrective action assigned, and repeat defects monitored.
