Executive breakdown

Eligibility is a time-sensitive control, not a one-time checkbox.

Active coverage is only the first layer

The workflow may also need item-specific benefits, cost share, authorization, rental or purchase rules, frequency limits, and network information.

Evidence must remain connected

Eligibility should connect to the order, supporting documentation, proof of delivery, continued use or need, refill controls, and the final claim path.

Recurring HME requires recurring control

Coverage and payer order may change after initial delivery. Re-verification triggers help prevent stale information from driving later claims.

Eligibility is not a guarantee

A payer response supports administrative readiness but does not guarantee that a future claim will be reimbursed.

Eligibility-to-closure chain

The first verification should not silently control every future claim.

Simulated case

The coverage that was correct at delivery but wrong at month four.

A simulated HME supplier verifies active commercial coverage and secondary Medicare information before delivering a rented positive-airway-pressure device. Three months later, the employment coverage terminates. The recurring billing workflow continues using the original payer order because no re-verification trigger or COB review is configured.

Visible issueThe monthly rental claim is unpaid and replacement supplies are placed on hold.First loss of controlThe recurring workflow treated initial eligibility and payer order as permanent instead of time-sensitive data.Why it was missedThe system confirmed claim creation, but it did not confirm current coverage, COB order, or recurring requirements.Patient impactPossible supply delay, fear of losing coverage, repeated calls, and concern about an unexpected balance.Operational responseRe-verification triggers, COB reconciliation, item-specific readiness, owned exceptions, patient communication, synthetic UAT, KPIs, and verified closure.
Operational controls

From one-time verification to continuous eligibility control.

Re-verification triggers

Check coverage before initial delivery, recurring rental billing, replacement, new supply cycles, and known insurance-change events.

Item-specific readiness

Separate active coverage from benefit, authorization, order, medical record, rental, frequency, and delivery requirements.

COB and exception ownership

Document payer order and route conflicting or failed verifications to named owners, due dates, and escalation paths.

Patient and account safeguards

Explain administrative holds, protect disputed balances, correct the claim path, and confirm downstream closure.

Eligibility is not complete when the payer responds. It is complete when the information is interpreted, connected to item requirements, acted upon, and verified downstream.
