I am a Bachelor of Science in Health Administration student building a professional portfolio at the intersection of healthcare operations, health informatics, and workflow analysis. My approach is grounded in something I learned by sitting with patients and families during some of their hardest moments. Confusion, delay, and frustration are rarely personal failures. They are almost always information failures.

That belief shapes everything I build. Weak handoffs, inconsistent documentation, unclear ownership, and preventable denials are not random. They are patterns, and patterns can be studied, structured, and improved. My portfolio exists to show how I turn real healthcare experience into informatics thinking that is careful, credible, and genuinely useful.
