Case Studies

UT Medical Center · Patient Experience

Translating Patient Feedback into Data Signals

Front-line patient experience work surfaced recurring themes in communication clarity, wait-time transparency, and care ownership.

Read full case study

Problem: Patient feedback was abundant but unstructured — scattered across conversations, notes, and surveys, with recurring complaints about communication clarity, wait-time uncertainty, and confusion about care ownership.

Approach: I grouped recurring themes into measurable categories and mapped them to workflow touchpoints such as admission, discharge documentation, and staff handoffs.

Impact: Turned qualitative feedback into trackable signals that inform quality improvement and informatics initiatives.

Remote Informatics Value: Demonstrates data framing and workflow awareness directly transferable to remote Health Informatics Analyst work.

Patricia Neal Rehabilitation Center · Patient Experience

Mapping Rehab Transitions & Documentation Gaps

Inpatient rehab transitions revealed high-risk points where documentation and patient understanding diverged.

Read full case study

Problem: Patients and families struggled to understand rehab goals, discharge plans, and care ownership across transitions from acute care to rehab to home.

Approach: Mapped the rehab journey step by step and linked recurring patient questions to specific documentation touchpoints.

Impact: Identified transitions of care as high-risk points for data breakdown.

Remote Informatics Value: Demonstrates transition mapping and care coordination thinking.
