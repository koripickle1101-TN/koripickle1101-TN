Case Studies

UT Medical Center · Patient Experience

Translating Patient Feedback into Data Signals

Front-line patient experience work surfaced recurring themes in communication clarity, wait-time transparency, and care ownership. This case study shows how I translated unstructured feedback into informatics-ready categories tied to real workflow touchpoints.

Read full case study

Problem: Patient feedback was abundant but unstructured — scattered across conversations, notes, and surveys, with recurring complaints about communication clarity, wait-time uncertainty, and confusion about care ownership.

Approach: I grouped recurring themes into measurable categories — communication clarity, wait-time transparency, and navigation/ownership — and mapped them to workflow touchpoints such as admission, discharge documentation, and staff handoffs.

Impact: This work demonstrated how qualitative front-line data can be structured into trackable signals that inform quality improvement and informatics initiatives, including standardized patient education fields and clearer discharge templates.

Remote Informatics Value: Demonstrates data framing, workflow awareness, and the ability to connect patient experience themes to formal quality and informatics measures — skills directly transferable to remote Health Informatics Analyst work.

Patricia Neal Rehabilitation Center · Patient Experience

Mapping Rehab Transitions & Documentation Gaps

Inpatient rehab transitions revealed high-risk points where documentation and patient understanding diverged. This case study maps the rehab journey as a data and communication workflow, identifying where information gets lost between care teams.

Read full case study

Problem: Patients and families struggled to understand rehab goals, discharge plans, and who owned which part of their care — pointing to documentation gaps across transitions from acute care to rehab to home.

Approach: I mapped the rehab journey step by step — admission, therapy, discharge planning, follow-up — and linked recurring patient questions to specific documentation touchpoints and communication gaps.

Impact: Identified transitions of care as high-risk points for data breakdown, and highlighted the value of standardized, patient-friendly documentation to improve continuity of care.

Remote Informatics Value: Demonstrates transition mapping, documentation alignment, and care coordination thinking — directly applicable to remote data quality review and workflow design roles.
